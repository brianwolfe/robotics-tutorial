Particle Filter

The particle filter attempts to represent the probability distribution directly through sampling. Regions of higher probability are populated with more particles. Regions of lower probability are depleted of particles. The particle filter has several important properties:
  • Unlike the Gaussian filters, no assumption is made on the form of the distribution. A particle filter can often track multimodal, complex distributions more accurately than a Gaussian filter if enough particles are used.
  • The computational cost and accuracy can be traded off: more particles will give a more accurate depiction of the true distribution, but will take more storage and computational power to track.
The 2.5D robot on a track demo is shown below. Particles are represented by small semi-transparent dots with lines indicating the heading. The mean and covariance of the particles are shown as a covariance ellipse with the mean heading in dark blue and the one standard deviation depicted in light blue. Change the encoder readings and click move to propagate forward. After each movement step, each particle is propagated forward by the amount indicated by the encoder readings plus some sampled that is distributed according to the matrix $Q$. Click on the plot to sample a measurement at a particular location. The measurement will resample the particles, weighted by the relative likelihood derived from Bayes rule.
